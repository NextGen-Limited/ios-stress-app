# When Will StressMonitor Prompt "Stress Overload"?

## What Types of Stress Can StressMonitor Detect?

There are three types of stress: **physical stress** (the body's fight-or-flight response), **emotional stress** (feelings like sadness or anxiety), and **cognitive stress** (mental challenges like difficulty concentrating).

StressMonitor specifically detects **physical stress** using HRV and resting heart rate data. Higher HRV and lower resting heart rate indicate a relaxed body; lower HRV and higher resting heart rate suggest tension.

The app acknowledges that emotional and cognitive stress can influence physical responses, but it cannot comprehensively detect all stress types.

## When Is Physical Stress Likely to Be High?

Several factors elevate physical stress readings:

- High mental stress (significantly lowers HRV)
- Physical fatigue from poor sleep or overexertion
- Illness (HRV decreases during sickness)
- Dehydration
- Unhealthy or irregular eating patterns
- Alcohol, caffeine, and tobacco use
- During or immediately after exercise

## Data Update Notes

**HRV Data:** Apple Watch automatically measures HRV every 2–5 hours under normal conditions. Measurements pause during Low Power Mode, exercise, high-speed movement, when the watch is locked, or with outdated watchOS versions.

**Real-time Stress Data:** Updates approximately every 15–30 minutes under normal conditions, subject to the same restrictions.

## Three Things to Keep in Mind

1. **HRV ≠ mental stress.** HRV reflects physical state — it's also influenced by fatigue, stimulants, age, and genetics.

2. **Observe trends, not single readings.** HRV naturally fluctuates throughout the day; multiple measurements over time give more accurate insights.

3. **Personalization takes time.** Accuracy improves after 30 days of consistent Apple Watch usage as your baseline stabilizes.

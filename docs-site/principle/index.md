# What Type of Stress Does StressMonitor Measure?

StressMonitor measures **physical stress based on heart rate data** — specifically Heart Rate Variability (HRV) and resting heart rate.

## What is Physical Stress?

Physical stress reflects whether the body's autonomic nervous system is under tension. It is triggered by many factors: psychological pressure, fatigue, illness, poor sleep, dehydration, and consumption of alcohol or caffeine. Prolonged elevated physical stress can lead to weakened immunity, cardiovascular risks, anxiety, and sleep problems.

## Physical Stress vs. Psychological Stress

Physical stress and psychological stress are positively correlated in many cases. When people experience acute or chronic psychological pressure, HRV and resting heart rate change in measurable ways.

However, they don't always align — alcohol or caffeine creates physical stress even when you feel mentally relaxed. Physical stress is objective; psychological stress involves subjective perception.

## What Can Monitoring Physical Stress Tell You?

- Detect elevated physical stress early through HRV and resting heart rate trends
- Gain insight into how lifestyle factors (sleep, exercise, diet) affect your body
- Identify and prevent potential health risks proactively
- Guide stress management decisions with objective data

## How StressMonitor Measures Physical Stress

The autonomic nervous system controls the heart through two branches: the sympathetic nervous system (triggers stress responses) and the parasympathetic nervous system (enables rest and recovery). Stress produces measurable changes in HRV and resting heart rate.

StressMonitor analyzes your individual HRV and resting heart rate against your **personal historical baseline** — not population averages — to produce a personalized stress assessment in four levels:

| Level | Color | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| Excellent State | 🟢 Green | High HRV, low resting HR — very low body pressure |
| Normal State | 🔵 Blue | Normal HRV and resting HR — manageable pressure |
| Attention Needed | 🟡 Yellow | Low HRV or elevated HR — consider rest |
| Pressure Overload | 🔴 Red | Significant HRV drop and HR spike — elevated health risk |

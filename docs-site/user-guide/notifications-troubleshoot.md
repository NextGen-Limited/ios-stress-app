# Watch & iPhone Notification Issues

Due to inherent delays in the Apple Watch system, occasional notification delays are normal. If you're consistently not receiving stress alerts, check the following:

## Checklist

**1. Verify StressMonitor is installed on your Watch**

Open the Watch app on your iPhone → My Watch → scroll to find StressMonitor → confirm it's installed and shown as installed on watch.

**2. Enable notifications on iPhone**

Go to **Settings → StressMonitor → Notifications** and confirm notifications are enabled.

**3. Enable notifications for Watch**

Open the **Watch** app on iPhone → Notifications → StressMonitor → confirm alerts are allowed.

**4. Check special modes**

Ensure your iPhone or Apple Watch is not in Low Power Mode, Sleep mode, Focus mode, Do Not Disturb, Theater mode, or Workout mode. These all suppress notifications.

**5. Enable Background App Refresh on iPhone**

Go to **Settings → General → Background App Refresh** and confirm it's enabled, including for StressMonitor specifically (check both the master toggle and the per-app setting).

**6. Enable Background App Refresh on Apple Watch**

On your Apple Watch: **Settings → General → Background App Refresh** → ensure it's on for StressMonitor.

## If None of the Above Works

Contact support at **support@stressmonitor.app** with:
- Your iPhone and Apple Watch models
- iOS and watchOS version numbers

We'll investigate and aim to address it in a future update.

## Notifications Arriving Too Frequently

Reduce frequency in StressMonitor → Settings → Notifications → Notification Intensity.

The minimum interval between overload alerts is 4 hours.

## Notifications Arriving Late

Background refresh timing is managed by iOS based on your usage patterns and battery. StressMonitor cannot guarantee exact delivery timing.

For time-sensitive monitoring, open the app manually for a current reading.
